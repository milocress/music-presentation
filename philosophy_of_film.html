Good-making and evaluative bases

              
H&M argue that a film's "good-making" features are those
that contribute to the aesthetic value of the film. This 
is similar to Carrol's idea of the evaluative basis of a 
film. We may only be able to compare films within the 
same genre, but we do so by comparing their relative 
aesthetic qualities.
